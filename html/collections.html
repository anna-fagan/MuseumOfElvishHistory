Verdant Blade (“Sylveneth”)

Mithril-steel alloy and living-wood composite, c. 2,800 years (Late Era of Confluence)

Recovered from a forest barrow near the Eryndor rivers, this luminous blade embodies the Confluence ideal of harmony between forge and forest. Its silver-green surface, veined with mineralized root tissue that glows under moonlight, reveals a craftsmanship where metal and wood were fused through a ritual process now lost to history.

Microscopic traces of resin and sap along the edge, and the absence of combat wear, suggest its role in ceremonies of renewal rather than war—ritual pruning, consecration, or stewardship. The blade’s inscriptional silence speaks through form: growth shaped by restraint.

In Reflective Interpretation Sessions, the Verdant Blade is paired with Eryndor forest windscapes and fragments of ancient verse, inviting visitors to experience the Elvish belief that even a weapon could become a vessel of care. Meaning, here, arises not from conquest—but from relationship.
